11.Wolfenbüttel Gardeners – Wolfenbütteler Gärtner

The profession of gardening had a special significance in the town's development. For a long time Wolfenbüttel was one of the largest vegetable-growing areas in northern Germany. The gardens outside the town gates were first mentioned in 1532, although these were chiefly kitchen gardens for self-sufficiency. Not until the early 18th century can we speak of commercial horticulture. Large areas were now cultivated: not only lettuce, peas and cabbage but also asparagus, artichokes and broccoli were grown.
